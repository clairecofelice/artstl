Composed of intersecting linear elements, the brightly colored 49-foot-tall sculpture

is visible from both an elevated highway and the streetscape below.

02

Observe its silhouette.

What does it resemble?

In designing this site-specific work, Cooper incorporated several defining

elements of the Landing and St. Louis history into an abstract 3-dimensional work. These themes include the historic river and land routes that converge in
St. Louis as well as its history as a jazz capital. To emphasize the role of jazz, Cooper designed the sculpture to resemble a giant abstracted treble clef.

Can you recognize elements of the treble clef in Cooper's sculpture?

03

Get close to the sculpture

and look at its textures.

The piece is fabricated from copper and mild steel painted with powder coat

enamel the most durable of paint treat- ments. It has a clear coat added to prevent scratches deep enough to mar the color. Reflective highway paint was used on one linear element. A final clear-coat varnish offers protection and allows any graffiti to be easily removed.
